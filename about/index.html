About

IEEE

IEEE is an acronym for Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers. IEEE was formed when AIEE (American Institute of Electrical Engineers) and IRE (Institute of Radio Engineers) came together on 1 January 1963. At its formation, IEEE had 150,000 members who were electrical and electronics engineers.

Members

Now being the world's largest technical professional organization, it consists of students pursuing technical courses, computer scientists, software developers, information technology professionals, physicists, medical doctors, and many others in addition to IEEE's electrical and electronics engineering core.

IEEE Regions

IEEE, having 339 sections in 10 geographical regions, is governed by a body of elected members. Every year the voting members of IEEE elects a president (currently Mr. Toshio Fukuda) who serves for 3 years, first as President-elect, then as CEO of IEEE and lastly as Past President.
